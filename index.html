🛬 Guangzhou — Arrival Day

✈️ Arrive 12:10 from Singapore • Oct 16

🎯 First Day Game Plan

Land at Baiyun Airport at 12:10, clear customs, grab SIM cards, and head to the hotel. You've got a relaxed afternoon and evening to explore before the morning train to Yangshuo tomorrow.

🏨 Where We Stay

LangYue Hotel (Hedong East Subway Station) — No. 10 Fulu Street, Liwan District. Opened 2025, clean rooms with projection screens, near metro. Alternative: Banana Fun Hotel near Ergong Subway Station, Haizhu District.

🍜 Dim Sum Dinner

Guangzhou = birthplace of dim sum. Head to Diǎn Dū Dé (点都德) for the most authentic har gow, siu mai, char siu bao, and rice rolls you'll ever taste. Budget about ¥80-100/person for a feast.

🚶 Beijing Road Walking Street

A 500m pedestrian shopping street with ancient glass-covered ruins beneath your feet — 2,000-year-old road foundations preserved under transparent panels. Great for snacks, tea, and getting your first taste of Chinese urban energy. The area dates back to the Tang Dynasty and is one of Guangzhou's oldest commercial districts.

⛰️ Yangshuo — Karst Paradise

🚄 HSR Guangzhou 09:00 → Yangshuo 11:30 • Oct 17-18

🏨 Where We Stay

West Street (Xi Jie) area — The heart of Yangshuo's backpacker scene. Cobblestone streets lined with cafes, bars, and shops. Stay close to the action but expect noise at night.

Day 1 (Oct 17) — Countryside E-bike Ride

Pedal through the Ten-Mile Gallery as karst peaks tower on both sides. The Yulong River winds alongside rice paddies — you'll pass ancient stone bridges and local farmers tending their fields. Rent an e-bike for ¥50-80/day from West Street. Best time: late afternoon when the light turns golden and mist settles between the peaks.

Day 2 (Oct 18) — Li River Bamboo Rafting

Drift on a traditional bamboo raft through the Xingping section — the exact landscape printed on the Chinese 20 yuan note. The 1.5-hour ride passes through dramatic gorges with cormorant fishermen and water buffalo along the banks. Afterward, climb Laozhai Hill (20 mins up, 1,500 steps) for a 360° panorama of peaks disappearing into mist. Evening: West Street dinner before the morning train to Chengdu.

🐼 Chengdu — Pandas, Tea & Spice

🚄 HSR from Yangshuo (5.5h) • Oct 19-22

Giant Panda Research Base

Watch baby pandas tumble off logs, play-fight their siblings, and nap draped over tree branches. The morning session (before 10 AM) is when the cubs are most active. This alone makes Chengdu unforgettable.

People's Park & Kuanzhai Alley

Sip jasmine tea in a bamboo chair while locals practice tai chi around you. Then wander the restored Qing-dynasty lanes of Kuanzhai Alley — snack stalls, face-changing opera performers, and Sichuan pepper everything.

Chunxi Road — Neon Nights

Chengdu's Times Square. A vibrant pedestrian street packed with flagship stores, street performers, and endless food options. Best at night when the neon signs light up. Great for shopping and people-watching.

🏔️ Jiuzhaigou — Valley of Nine Villages

🚄 HSR from Chengdu • Oct 20-21 (whirlwind trip!)

🚄 Getting There (Oct 20)

Morning: People's Park tea, Kuanzhai Alley, quick hotpot lunch. Afternoon: HSR Chengdu → Jiuhuang (16:02-18:27 or 14:39-16:49, ~2.5h). From the station, shuttle bus or taxi (45 min) to the park entrance area.

🏨 Where We Stay

Lavande Hotel (Jiuzhaigou Tourist Center) — Huodiba, Longkang Village, Zhangzha Town. Rating 9.5/10. Right next to Guoda car park, walking distance to park entrance. Bianbian Street nearby for food. Free parking, service robots, clean rooms.

🗺️ The Y-Shaped Valley Strategy (Oct 21)

The park has a Y-shaped layout with 3 valleys. Pro tip: Take the shuttle bus to the TOP of Rize Valley first (Long Lake), then walk DOWN through the highlights. This saves energy and lets you see the best spots with fewer crowds. The park covers 72,000 hectares — you can't walk everything, so use the shuttle buses strategically.

Must-see route: Long Lake → Five Flower Lake (the crown jewel — submerged fallen trees in turquoise water) → Pearl Shoal Waterfall → Mirror Lake → Nuorilang Waterfall (China's widest) → then explore Shuzheng Valley if time permits.

🍂 October = peak autumn foliage. Golden leaves reflecting in crystal-clear water. Arrive at 8 AM when gates open — the lakes are most mirror-like before crowds stir the water.

🚄 Return (Oct 21 Evening)

Full day in the park (08:00-17:00). Evening train back: HSR Jiuhuang → Chengdu (19:00-21:24 or 20:40-22:20 via Songpan). This is a rushed trip — ideally you'd spend 2 nights, but we're making it work!

🌃 Chongqing — The Cyberpunk City

🚄 HSR Chengdu 09:00 → Chongqing 10:30 • Oct 23-25

🏨 Where We Stay

Jiefangbei / Hongyadong area — Stay right on top of the action. Holiday Riverview Hotel has views of Qiansimen Bridge and costs ~$30/night. The area is busy day and night, but you're in the middle of all the magic.

Day 1 — Qiansimen Bridge, Hongyadong & Jiefangbei

Qiansimen Bridge: Walk across for the best elevated view of Chongqing's layered architecture — monorails sliding by, skyscrapers rising from cliffs. Hongyadong: 11-story building cascading down a cliff. At night, thousands of golden lanterns light up every level — it's straight out of Spirited Away. Enter from street level (top) or river (bottom). Jiefangbei: Like Times Square meets Tokyo's Shibuya — giant LED screens, malls, and endless food options.

Day 2 — Liziba, Shibati & Yangtze Cableway

Liziba Monorail Station: Watch a monorail pass THROUGH a residential building. Peak Chongqing absurdity — there's a purpose-built viewing platform below (20-30 min stop). Shibati (18 Stairs): Restored historical neighborhood showing old Chongqing before modernization. Steep staircases, preserved homes, food alleys, coffee shops, and views of the modern skyline. Yangtze Cableway: Cross the river in a vintage cable car — incredible city views.

Day 3 — 🏔️ Wulong Karst Day Trip

Getting there: Metro/Didi to Chongqing East Station → HSR to Wulong South (45 min, new station) → shuttle bus to Tourist Center (45 min, ¥16). Three Natural Bridges: Colossal stone arches (Tianlong, Qinglong, Heilong) spanning a misty valley, accessed via futuristic glass elevator. UNESCO site, filming location for Transformers 4 and Curse of the Golden Flower. Walk takes 2-3 hours. Warning: Book return train early — popular slots fill up fast!

🎬 Zhangjiajie — Avatar's Pandora

🚄 HSR from Chongqing (5h) • Oct 26-28

🏔️ Avatar Hallelujah Mountain

The real-life inspiration for Pandora's floating mountains. Thousand-meter sandstone pillars wrapped in clouds and clinging vegetation. James Cameron confirmed this was the visual reference for the film. Walking among the peaks feels like another planet.

Bailong Elevator

The world's tallest outdoor elevator — 326 meters up a sheer cliff face in a glass cabin. In 90 seconds you rise from the valley floor to the cloud-wrapped summit plateau. The view on the way up is staggering.

⛩️ Tianmen Mountain — Heaven's Gate

Take the world's longest cable car (7.5 km) from the city center straight up to the peak. Walk the glass skywalk bolted to the cliff face 1,400m above the valley. Then descend 999 steps through the natural arch of Heaven's Gate — a massive hole through the mountain.

🏯 Day Trip Options: Ancient Towns

The last afternoon can be spent visiting one of China's most beautiful ancient towns. Both are about 2-3 hours from Zhangjiajie. Choose based on your vibe!

🏮 Fenghuang Ancient Town

The "Phoenix Town" — more popular, more polished. Traditional Miao-style stilt houses line the Tuojiang River. Red lanterns reflect in the water at dusk. Stone bridges, riverside bars, and night markets. Best for: evening ambiance, boat rides, photography.

🌊 Furong Ancient Town

The "Hibiscus Town" — less touristy, more dramatic. A 60-meter waterfall crashes through the center of town. Traditional Tujia stilt houses perched on cliff edges. Features in the classic 1980s Chinese film "Hibiscus Town." Best for: waterfall views, authentic feel, fewer crowds.

✈️ Guangzhou & Shenzhen — Start and Finish

✈️ Arrival Oct 16 • 🚄 Return via HSR Oct 29-31

Guangzhou — Dim Sum Capital of the World

The birthplace of Cantonese cuisine. Start mornings with yum cha — steaming bamboo baskets of har gow (crystal shrimp dumplings), siu mai, char siu bao, and silky congee. The food alone is worth the stop.

Shenzhen Day Trip (Oct 30)

30 minutes by train from Guangzhou. A futuristic tech city that went from fishing village to megacity in 40 years. OCT-LOFT creative park, Lianhuashan Park for skyline views, and the Futian light show at night.

🛍️ What to Buy in China

Best buys based on Reddit r/travel + r/Chinavisa recommendations

📱 Electronics & Tech

DJI Drones & Cameras — 15-20% cheaper than US/India. DJI flagship stores in Guangzhou and Shenzhen. Mini 4 Pro, Osmo Action 5, Pocket 3 are popular picks.

Xiaomi Products — Mi Bands, electric toothbrushes, smart home gadgets at fraction of export prices. Xiaomi Home stores everywhere.

Huaqiangbei Market (Shenzhen) — The world's largest electronics market. Everything from phone accessories to components. Great for cables, chargers, cases.

⚠️ Skip: iPhones (same price globally), laptops (Chinese keyboard layout).

👗 Fashion & Clothing

Silk — Hangzhou is the silk capital, but Chengdu and Guangzhou have good shops. Scarves, pajamas, bedding at great prices.

Cashmere — Inner Mongolia cashmere is world-class. Look for 100% cashmere sweaters. Erdos brand is reliable.

Tailored Suits — Custom suits in 24-48 hours. Shenzhen and Guangzhou markets have tailors who copy any design.

Outdoor Gear — Local brands (Kailas, Toread) offer quality hiking gear at 30-50% of Western brands.

🍵 Tea & Food

Chinese Tea — Longjing (Dragon Well) from Hangzhou, Pu'er from Yunnan, Tieguanyin oolong. Buy from specialty tea shops, not tourist markets.

Sichuan Peppercorns — The numbing spice that defines Sichuan cuisine. Get them vacuum-sealed in Chengdu.

White Rabbit Candy — Classic Chinese milk candy. Nostalgia in a wrapper. Great gifts.

Chili Oil / Lao Gan Ma — Stock up on crispy chili oil. Heavy but worth it.

🎨 Culture & Souvenirs

Chinese Calligraphy Brushes — Lake Pen brushes (Huzhou) are prized. Also ink stones and rice paper.

Cloisonné — Traditional enamel work on copper. Beijing is famous, but available nationwide.

Jade & Pearls — Only buy from certified shops. Fake jade is everywhere. Freshwater pearls are affordable.

Panda Merch — In Chengdu, the official Panda Base shop has quality items. Skip the random tourist stalls.

💡 Pro Tips: Always bargain in markets (start at 30% of asking price). Use WeChat Pay for better deals. Keep receipts for expensive items. Declare electronics at customs if over ₹50,000 total.

Practical Info

South (Guangzhou, Yangshuo): 20-28°C — summer clothes + light layer

Central (Chengdu, Chongqing): 15-22°C — comfortable, light jacket

Mountains (Jiuzhaigou, Zhangjiajie): 5-15°C — warm fleece + rain jacket

October = golden autumn. Peak foliage in mountains.
